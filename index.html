Project Name

Write some love.

Carpooling is a simple and effective method to reduce traffic and the pollution it creates. Presently however there has yet to be widespread adoption of the practice. The greatest challenge in expanding the use of carpooling remains connecting drivers and passengers, and this is what mRides seeks to change.

We investigated two carpooling apps, Netlift and ToGo, in order to understand why carpooling was not gaining popularity with the rise of mobile use. We found that, on one hand, Netlift had a very intuitive user interface, with pertinent information easy to retrieve and displayed in a consise fashion. Despite this, Netlift was awarded a meager 3 stars out of 5 on the Google Play Store due to its many bugs and lack of advanced features.

On the other hand, ToGo sports chat between passengers and drivers and provides details about a user's preferences, features which Netlift lacks. ToGo's principal shortcomings are that it is single platform (Android) and has a substandard user interface. Our goal with mRides is to packaged the advanced features found in apps like ToGo in a stylish and intuitive user interface similar to Netlift.

Team Epsilon believes that "it just works" is never enough. Our apps fuse function with style to create a seamless experience our users enjoy and remember.

Our choice of platform for both our bids is Ionic. We have extensive experience crafting beautiful web applications with HTML5, CSS, and JavaScript, including the Angular JavaScript library. Please see RoomM8 and AnotherOne for examples of web sites that we have created. Furthermore, one of our team members has already created a cross-platform mobile app with Ionic. This gives us the technical advantage to begin development of our app the moment we are awarded our choice.

We do our research

The proliferation of smartphones and internet services have spurred the rapid growth of the sharing economy. In particular, ridesharing, which was a relatively unknown concept has been thrust into the spotlight thanks to apps such as Uber and Lyft. This part of the report aims to detail and analyze applications that are similar to mRides in an attempt to better understand the market for this application and identify any unique features that can be implemented. In particular, this part of the report will focus on two mobile carpooling apps, which are Netlift and Togo Carpooling.

mRides Competition Analysis

Netlift

Introduction-NetLift

A carpooling app founded in Montreal, Netlift is an app which seeks to use empty space in vehicles to transport other passengers around the area. This is an app where an individual who is using it, may become a driver or a passenger. The app also provides the ability to rate drivers as well as passengers and to view other data such as ride history and community ratings. The pricing for the application is as follows:

(pricing charts for Netlift)

Observing the interface of Netlift, the app seems to be extremely similar to other ridesharing and carpooling apps.

(In app ios screen shots of Netlift)

The app features a simple to use map which tracks the user location as well as the destination. This app has a clear and concise area for displaying an individual’s pending carpool reservation and matches. The bottom portion of the screen has lower tabs that display home and news.

Analysis-Netlift

While the app does have a very good design and interface, it appears to have a very mediocre rating on the Google Play Store. With a rating of 3 out of 5 stars, the most common complaint appears to be a lack of technical support. There also appears to be a wide range of bugs experienced by the users who do use the application.

Another potential weakness of this app is that it appears to be very Quebec centric. It offers details of insurance plans in Quebec for individuals who carpool. The same benefits may not be applicable to all states where this app might be used however. Other countries or states may not be so generous with their insurance plans. This may cause drivers to possibly move away from the app or pickup less passengers(less liability)

ToGo Carpool

Introduction-ToGo

ToGo Carpool is another application with an emphasis on carpooling in both the App Store and Google Play Store. This app performs similar functions to the Netlift app, in that it provides a platform for drivers and passengers to connect and for drivers to bring them to their requested location.

(In App screenshots of the ToGo mobile app)

The app features the ability to provide details about your preferences from the get go, as evidenced by the in app screenshots. In addition, the app also appears to give the user the ability to participate in group chat with the driver and other passengers. The ability to chat with other drivers in a different carpooling group exists as well.

Analysis-ToGo

The application appears to favor Google’s material design over Netlift’s flat UI design. This could be due to the fact that this app is only available on the Google Play store. As a result, a significant portion of the population using ios devices are being alienated. This is an extremely large weakness in the app. Furthermore, the groupchat feature in the app appears to be extremely useful in a carpooling app.

Like the Netlift app being focused in the Quebec region, it appears that the Togo Mobile app has a particular preference to India. With the main office branch in Mumbai, most of the app’s reviews also appear to come from Indians whom have used the app themselves. It appears that the ToGo mobile app is targeting Large cities within India.

Conclusions

The aim of this part of the report is to observe how other apps which are similar to mRides are being implemented. There are many carpooling apps available in the store, thus it was important to find find carpooling apps which stand more apart from the rest. The Netlift mobile app has a beautiful design while the ToGo mobile app has some features other carpooling apps should have, but don’t. The aim of this part is to help facilitate coming up with beneficial functional requirements that our application may incorporate upon release.

TESTING FRAMEWORKS

The core of our Ionic project will be written in angular js, there exist many frameworks to test angular apps. For our project, we will be working with Jasmine which is one of the most popular frameworks for providing testing within angular apps. Coupled with Jasmine our team will also use Karma which is a tool built by Angular for running unit tests.

The backend unit testing will be done with JUnit which is a popular unit testing framework for the Java language. UI tests will most likely be done manually but if time permits and all features are finished, we will utilize the Selenium framework to automate our UI tests.
